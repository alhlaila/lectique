Wanderer

Above the Sea of Fog

Caspar
David
Friedrich

"I HAVE TO STAY FULLY ALONE IN ORDER TO FULLY CONTEMPLATE AND FEEL NATURE"

Gallery

In this evocative painting, a solitary figure stands atop a rocky pinnacle, gazing out at a breathtaking Alpine vista. Surrounding peaks emerge from a sea of mist, while a distant mountain looms against a luminous sky.

Friedrich drew inspiration from his time in Switzerland for this scene, though the thick fog shrouds the landscape, leaving much to the imagination.

>

Positioned centrally, the figure becomes the focal point, with the horizontal lines of the rocks and distant slopes directing attention towards him.

>

The stark contrast between his dark silhouette and the pale hues of the fog and sky heightens the impact of the image.

While possibly intended as a posthumous tribute to a Saxon Infantry colonel—depicted here in a heroic stance—the painting lends itself to various interpretations.

It can symbolize humanity's longing for the unattainable or serve as an allegory for life's journey. Reflecting Romantic ideals, it encapsulates the individual's isolation when confronted with the sublime power of nature, thus becoming an iconic representation of the Romantic spirit.
